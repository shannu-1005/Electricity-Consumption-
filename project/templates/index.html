📋 Problem Understanding

Electricity is a vital driver of economic activity, and its consumption reflects both industrial productivity and household energy access. In 2015–16, agriculture accounted for 17.89% of electric energy usage in India. Despite relatively low electricity tariffs, India's per capita electricity consumption remains below global averages, indicating both growth potential and infrastructural challenges.


The COVID-19 pandemic introduced a unique scenario for energy analysis. With lockdowns enforced, various sectors saw drastic shifts — industries ↓ transportation ↓ residential ↑ — creating a once-in-a-generation dataset for understanding demand elasticity and economic coupling.

⚙️ Project Methodology

📌 Problem Definition

  • Analyze electricity consumption shifts during COVID-19
  • Identify regional demand disparities across 5 regions
  • Quantify lockdown impact on industrial vs. residential sectors
  • Measure recovery speed by state post-lockdown

🗃️ Data Collection & DB

  • Monthly state-wise consumption data (MU) for 2019–2020
  • Stored in SQLite database (electricity.db)
  • SQL operations: INSERT, GROUP BY, JOIN, VIEW, filters
  • Calculation fields: YoY %, Recovery Index, Regional Share

🎨 Data Preparation & Viz

  • Pandas for data cleaning & transformation
  • 6 unique visualizations via Chart.js
  • Interactive filters: State, Region, Year, Month range
  • Dashboard + Data Story (5 scenes) embedded in Flask

🌐 Web Integration

  • Flask REST API with 8 endpoints
  • Dashboard embedded at /dashboard
  • Story view embedded at /story
  • Fully responsive premium dark UI
