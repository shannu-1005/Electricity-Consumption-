The Pandemic Hits
On March 24, 2020, India entered one of the world's strictest nationwide lockdowns. Within weeks, electricity demand collapsed — factories shut, offices emptied, and transportation froze. April 2020 saw consumption fall by over 35–40% compared to April 2019 in industrial states.

The Regional Divide
India's five regions experienced the pandemic's electricity impact very differently. Western states (Maharashtra, Gujarat) with high industrial bases saw the steepest falls. Northeastern states, with primarily agricultural and residential consumption, were far more resilient.

The Road to Recovery
As India's Unlock phases began in June 2020, electricity demand began a slow but steady rebound. By October 2020, most states had recovered to 95–98% of their 2019 levels — but the pace varied dramatically across states based on industrial composition and economic structure.

Scene 5 of 5
Lessons & Future Outlook
The 2019–2020 electricity data provides invaluable lessons for India's energy policy. The pandemic revealed India's electricity-economy coupling — and the resilience of agricultural and residential demand even during extreme disruptions. These insights should guide smarter grid infrastructure, demand forecasting, and energy equity policies.
